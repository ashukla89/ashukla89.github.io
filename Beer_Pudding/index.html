Western and Midwestern Breweries are the Best

But breweries just keep opening nationwide.

By Aseem Shukla (but shamelessly ripped off from the Pudding)

Published September 17, 2019

Beer is good. Some people really like beer. But who has the best beer, and has rising interest in this bubbly barleyed beverage peaked?

To answer these questions, the Pudding (i.e. definitely not Me) went and found RateBeer.com data and created a metric of beer city quality by weighting Quality of brews (as measured by reviews) against the Quantity of beers made by the breweries of that city (given a minimum and maximum number of available beers and reviews per brewery).

Using an 80%-20% quality-quantity weighting, Santa Rosa, CA emerges as the number 1 beer city. And perhaps unsurprisingly to beer aficionados, the top 10 list of beer cities is rounded out by other places on the West Coast, the Mountain West, or the Midwest.

But even as beer-brewing continues to spur many would-be Sam Adamses into action, the propensity of thinking people to start fermenting grain seems to differ by state. As an example, the good people of California, while generating the greatest number of new breweries annually, fall well behind those of Vermont, whose denizens found breweries at a higher (and as of 2016, still growing) rate than those of any other state.
